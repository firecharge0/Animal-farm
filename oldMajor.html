Old Major

Old major is an allegory for Karl Marx and Vladimir Lenin. He is portrayed as an old pig with around 400 piglets. He is respected by all the members on the farm.

Old Majors role in the book is to spread the idea of the revolution and to be a leader during the begining. He gives a speech in where he tell them about a dream in where he remembered a song from his childhood called "Beasts of England", and uses it to motivate the other animals of the farm. "Beasts of England" soon becomes the song of the revolution.

"Man. Man is the only real enemy we have. Remove Man from the scene, and the root cause of hunger and overwork is abolished for ever."
