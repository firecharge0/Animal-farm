Boxer

Boxer is an allegory for the ignorant working class. He works tirelessly for the farm and Napoleon being the "idol" of the farm. Eventually, despite how much he worked for the farm, when he bacame ill he was sold in order to purchase alchohol for the pigs. His main roll in the story is to show the dangers of unquestioning loyalty as in the end he is betrayed despite his two maxims.

"I will work harder" and "Napoleon's always right".
